About Me

My name is Tuan-Minh Nguyen (Vietnamese: Nguyễn Tuấn Minh). I am currently a Research Fellow in Probability Theory at the School of Mathematics, Monash University, Australia.

I was born in Hue, Vietnam. I completed my PhD under the supervision of Professor Stanislav Volkov at Lund University, Sweden. Prior to that, I earned my undergraduate degree at Belarusian State University.

Research Interests

Probability theory is my primary area of research. I focus on random processes on graphs, particularly self-interacting random processes — stochastic models whose future behavior depends on both their current state and their history.

Key examples include:

  • Random walks with reinforcement (prefer returning to visited sites)
  • Random walks with repulsion (prefer exploring new sites)
  • Excited random walks (behavior changes with number of visits)

I am also interested in random graphs, percolation theory, branching processes, random games, random matrices, queueing theory, point processes, SPDEs, and Malliavin calculus.

Publications

I have published papers in journals such as Annals of Applied Probability, Stochastic Processes and their Applications, Annales de l'Institut Henri Poincaré, and others.
